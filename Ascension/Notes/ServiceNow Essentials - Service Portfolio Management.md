## Service Portfolio Management

**Service Portfolio Management** (SPM) provides a structured approach to:

- Define and categorize services
- Evaluate service value and performance
- Manage services through their entire life cycle

### Goals of SPM

- Ensure that services are relevant, high quality, and provide real value
- Offer a single pane perspective of portfolios, taxonomy, services, and service offerings. 
- Enable better decision making by giving leaders clear visibility into services. Providing detail to services being ordered, their status, and performance.

### What is a Service? 

A way of delivering value to customers by delivering desired outcomes. 

#### Two primary service types:

1. Business Services - services that support the business directly to have customer facing functions. 
	- **Ex.** Printer support, Filed Services, Clinical informatics
	  
2. Technical Services - provide the technical foundation required for operations. 
	- Network Connectivity, Cloud Hosting, Database Management

It is a group of offerings which in their summation accomplishes a business or technical need. 

### What is a Service Portfolio?

A service portfolio is a standardized way to view all services across Ascension portfolios. It guarantees effective:

- Planning
- Validation
- Release

It is like a department for a group of services (**Ex.** Infrastructure and Engineering)

### What is a Service Offering?

A service offering defines what a team delivers and the technology they support. It is an individual task which in it's combination with other offerings provide a service. 

### Roles for SPM

- **Service Owner** - creates, updates, and maintains services within a portfolio. 
- **Service Editor** - edit services and offerings that they own or are delegated.
- **Service Viewer** - view portfolios, services, and offerings. 

### Service Outages

- Unplanned - caused by hardware failures, network issues, system/app malfunctions
- Planned - system maintenance, upgrades, or testing
- Degradation - reduction is performance. Slower response time, intermittent connectivity, and limited functionality.
