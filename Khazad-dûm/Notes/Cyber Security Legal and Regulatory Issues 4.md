## Privacy

**U.S.** - A patchwork of laws with not much actual protection. America does not give much regard to privacy. 

**Europe** - Europe is very pro-privacy. They provide strict laws on what data is gathered and stored. This creates problems and lawsuits for U.S. companies. 

### Laws you should know for the exam:

 **HIPAA** - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. Strict regulation on PHI (Protected Health Information). 

**Security Breach Notification Laws** - On the state level, not federal. 

**Electronic Communications Privacy Act** - Protection against warantless wire tapping. Weakened by patriot act. 

**PATRIOT Act of 2001** - Gave law enforce many more rights to monitor citizens and the capability to do so. Allows search and seizure. 

**Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)** - The common law used to prosecute computer crimes. 

**Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act** - Applies to financial institutions. 

**Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002** - Directly related to accounting scandals in the 90's.

**Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI-DSS)** - The standard that applies to cardholder data. Requires merchants to adhere to security standards.
